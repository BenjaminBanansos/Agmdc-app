Conference voting eligibility
Who can vote at the Biannual Conference
All pastors holding a Certificate or Ordination credential (License to Preach holders do not vote)
One authorised lay representative per recognised church (meeting conditional membership requirements)
Church recognition determined by district process — minimum members, regular reporting, financial compliance
Election rules
Only ordained ministers are eligible to contest for any Executive Committee or Regional Director position
Regional Director elections are overseen by the Presbytery and ratified at Conference
Presbyter elections are held at section level, subject to regional oversight
Three-tier pastoral credential system
Level 1
License to Preach
Entry level. Permits preaching and limited ministry functions. Cannot vote at conference. Cannot contest elections.
Entry
Level 2
Certificate
Full pastoral duties. Can vote at biannual conference. Can issue marriage, baptism, and dedication certificates. Cannot contest elections.
Full pastoral
Level 3
Ordination
Typically 7–10 years of service. Based on performance, knowledge, and character evaluation. Required to contest any EC or leadership position.
Election eligible

Complaint escalation chain
1
Section level — first hearing
All complaints must be raised first at the section Presbyter. Section has authority to mediate, resolve, or escalate. Most disputes resolved here.
Presbyter presides
2
Regional level — appeal or escalation
If unresolved at section, Regional Director takes over. Can hear witnesses, examine records, and issue regional rulings. Handles inter-section or inter-church disputes.
Regional Director presides
3
Executive Committee — final district authority
Cases that cannot be resolved at regional level. EC has full disciplinary authority — including suspension, credential withdrawal, or church transfer orders.
EC full authority
4
Tribunal — for serious or legal matters
Constituted by EC for cases involving legal proceedings, criminal allegations, or matters requiring formal adjudication. Tribunal decisions may be escalated to national AG leadership or civil courts if necessary.
Formal tribunal
Digital portal enables pastors and churches to file complaints online. Each case gets a unique reference number and is trackable through the portal at each escalation stage.
